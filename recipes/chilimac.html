Chilimac Recipe
Our Signature Chilimac recipe is typically made in about 8 hours and requires a Crockpot. Alternatives can be found in grey sections.
Sign in to save or see other recipes!

First! The Chili ingredients...

  • 1 lbs. Ground Turkey
  • 1x can of Pinto Beans
  • 1x can of Dark Kidney Beans
  • 1x Can of Light Kidney Beans
  • 1x can of Black Beans
  • 2x Diced Bell peppers
  • 1x Diced Onion
  • 1x can of Corn Kernels
  • 1x can of Diced Tomatoes
  • 1x can of Tomato Paste
  • 1x packet of preferred Taco or Chili seasoning
  • 2x tbsp of salt
  • 2x tbsp of Pepper
  • 2x tbsp of Olive Oil

Making the Chili

  1. Place Ground Turkey, Diced Bell Peppers, Diced Onions, Salt, Pepper, and Olive oil into a pan
  2. Cook on Medium heat until turkey appears mostly done
  3. Grab your crockpot and add all ingredients into it, including everything canned and what you just cooked
  4. Stir and break up the meat if necessary in the crockpot until everything is mixed
  5. Place Crockpot on "LOW" for 8 Hours

Alternatively use a can of store bought chili!

Mac n Cheeze

  • 1 lbs. uncooked pasta shells
  • 4x cups whole milk
  • 8x Tablespoons (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1/2x cup all-purpose flour
  • 3x cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1x cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4x teaspoon dry mustard (optional)

Creating the Mac n Cheeze

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the pasta and cook it just until al dente, about 11 minutes.
  3. Drain the pasta then set it aside.
  4. In a medium saucepan set over medium heat, warm the milk until it is simmering then set it aside.
  5. In a large heavy-bottomed stock pot, melt the butter over medium heat.
  6. Once the butter has melted, whisk in the flour and cook the mixture, whisking constantly.
  7. Continue Whisking this mixture for about 3min until it turns light brown
  8. Remove the pot from the heat then slowly whisk the warm milk into the flour mixture. Continue whisking constantly to avoid any lumps.
  9. Put the pot over the stove at medium-high and cook the mixture (whisking constantly), until it is thick enough to coat a spoon. (aaround 2min)
  10. Add seasonings like salt, pepper, or hot sauce to your desired flavor.
  11. Stirr in the Parmesan and Cheddar until they are melted in.
  12. Stirr in the cooked pasta, Remove from heat, and move to next step.

Want it easy? Instead use store
bought Macaroni and Cheese!

Assembling the Chilimac!

  • This is the simplest and easiest part!
    If you so choose you can use additional toppings below:
  • 1x more of your favorite Shredded Cheese
  • 2x tbsp Siracha or Tapatio for more spice
  • 1x bag of Tortilla chips to dip in
  • 2x cups of Cooked and Diced Bacon

Get yourself a big pot or a bunch of bowls!

  1. Begin by placing the freshly made Mac N Cheese with the now 8 hours done chili near each other.
  2. Grab the biggest bowl in your house or a bunch of individual bowls.
  3. Begin scooping 1x part Mac N Cheeze followed by 1x part Chili into the large bowl or smaller bowls.
  4. try and keep the ratio of your Mac to chili at 50-50.
  5. Do so until bowls are not more than 80% full.
  6. If using individual bowls Serve immediately to your guest or prepare them as meal prep for tomorrow.
  7. If using large bowl make sure you stirr and get the Chilimac well mixed.
  8. Serve at your leisure m'lord and m'lady, enjoy!
